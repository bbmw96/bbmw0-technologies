Under the Hood

13 Analysis Engines

Each engine is purpose-built for a specific domain of AEC compliance and project intelligence. Together they form the most comprehensive automated compliance platform available.

📐
Structural Compliance Engine
Cross-references structural design parameters against Eurocode, BS EN 1990–1999, and national annexes to flag non-compliances automatically.
BS EN 1990 Eurocode National Annexes
🔥
Fire Safety Analysis Engine
Analyses fire compartmentation, means of escape, sprinkler requirements, and fire resistance ratings against Approved Document B and international equivalents.
ADB 2019 Fire Safety Order BS 9999
♿
Accessibility Compliance Engine
Checks designs for compliance with Approved Document M, BS 8300, and equivalent accessibility standards across all 7 supported countries.
Approved Document M BS 8300 Equality Act
🌍
IFC Geometry Parser
Reads and validates IFC 4.3 model data, extracting spatial geometry, property sets, and relationships for downstream compliance analysis.
IFC 4.3 OpenBIM ISO 16739
📊
BIM Maturity Assessment Engine
Scores project BIM maturity across Levels 0–3 using PAS 1192, ISO 19650, and EIR/BEP assessment frameworks.
ISO 19650 PAS 1192 Level 2
⚡
Energy Performance Engine
Evaluates energy performance compliance against Part L, MEES regulations, and equivalent ASEAN energy codes for Singapore, Malaysia, and Thailand.
Part L MEES SS 530
🤖
AI Risk Scoring Engine
Uses machine learning classification to assign risk scores to project elements, prioritising the most critical compliance gaps for project teams.
ML Classification Risk Matrix Priority Queue

NLP Regulatory Cross-Reference
Natural language processing engine that maps project specifications and drawing notes to the most relevant regulatory clauses across the standards library.
NLP DeepSeek AEC Semantic Search

Security Shield Engine

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

JZW BIM AI can read IFC (ISO 16739), DXF, and standard document formats including PDF, DOCX, XLSX, and CSV for project data import. For BIM authoring tool formats such as Revit (.rvt), ArchiCAD (.pln), and Navisworks (.nwd), the application requires an export to IFC 4.3 or DXF first — this is standard practice across all major BIM coordination platforms including Solibri and Navisworks themselves.
No. JZW BIM AI is built as an offline-first application — all 787 standards and 13 analysis engines operate entirely locally on your machine. An internet connection is only required for the initial download, licence activation, and optional Outlook integration. Your project data never leaves your device.
Version 5.8.0 covers 7 countries: United Kingdom, Singapore, Malaysia, Japan, China, Thailand, and Brunei Darussalam. The UK coverage is the most comprehensive, with over 210 standards including all Approved Documents (A through R), the full Eurocode suite, Building Safety Act 2022, CDM 2015, and ISO 19650. Expansion to further markets including the Middle East, Australia, and North America is planned for future versions.
Licences are perpetual — a one-time purchase grants you lifetime use of that version with no ongoing subscription fees. Upon purchase, you receive a unique licence key delivered via email, which you enter on first launch. The key is validated against a SHA-256 hashed database of 1,001 valid keys. The licence persists locally on your device and includes a periodic heartbeat check to prevent simultaneous use across unauthorised installations.
JZW BIM AI is currently available via direct download from this website. Microsoft Store availability is in progress — the application is currently undergoing code signing through Azure Trusted Signing and Partner Center certification. The Store version will be available in the near future. In the meantime, the direct download installer from the Download section is fully functional and safe.
JZW BIM AI requires Windows 10 or Windows 11 (64-bit), the .NET 8 Runtime, and the WebView2 Runtime (pre-installed on all Windows 11 devices). Minimum 4 GB RAM is required, with 8 GB recommended for analysing large IFC models. Approximately 500 MB of disk space is needed. No internet connection is required for standard operation.
